Fishing techniques in Guadeloupe

Trolling

Ideal technique for catching pelagic fish such as mahi-mahi and tuna. Use artificial lures or natural baits trolled behind the boat at moderate speed.

  • Optimal speed: 5-8 knots
  • Depth: Surface to 50m
  • Best time: Early morning or late afternoon

Jigging

Effective vertical technique for rocky bottoms. Let the jig sink to the bottom then retrieve it with jerks to imitate an injured fish.

  • Depth: 30-200m
  • Jig weight: 80-200g depending on depth
  • Target species: Groupers, snappers, jacks

Throw-Bring Back

Perfect for coastal areas and shallow reefs. Cast your lure and retrieve it with a variety of actions.

  • Lures : Poppers, stickbaits, jerkbaits
  • Areas: Reefs, seagrass beds, mangroves
  • Species: Barracudas, jacks, tarpons

Bottom Fishing

A traditional and highly effective technique for reef fish. Use several hooks mounted on a main line with natural bait.

  • Bait: Sardines, squid, crabs
  • Assembly: 2-5 hooks spaced 50 cm apart
  • Depth: 15-80m

Deep-sea fishing

For marlins, giant tuna, and sharks. Requires sturdy equipment and good preparation.

  • Equipment: 50-130 lb rods, lever reels
  • Bait: Ballyhoo, bonito, surface lures
  • Best season: April to July for marlin

Fish Species of the Caribbean

Mahi-Mahi (Dorado)

Size : 50-150cm
Habitat : Pelagic waters, floating wrecks
Technique : Trolling, casting and retrieving
Season : All year round, peak March-June

Yellowfin Tuna

Size : 80-200cm
Habitat : Open sea, schools of fish
Technique : Fast trolling, jigging
Season : February-August

Barracuda

Size : 60-150cm
Habitat : Reefs, coastal areas
Technique : Casting and retrieving, coastal trolling
Season : All year round

Vivaneau

Size : 30-80cm
Habitat : Rocky bottom 30-150m
Technique : Bottom Fishing, jigging
Season : All year round

Thazard

Size : 100-250cm
Habitat : Open sea, occasionally coastal areas
Technique : Jigging, heavy trolling
Season : All year round

Jack (Crevalle)

Size : 40-120cm
Habitat :Reefs, channels, shipwrecks
Technique : Jigging, casting, bottom fishing
Season : All year round

Best Fishing Spots

Pigeon Islets (Cousteau Reserve)

Type : Protected coral reef
Depth : 5-40m
Species : Snappers, jacks, barracudas
Access : Boat only from Bouillante
Note : Regulated fishing, compliance with mandatory marine reserve

The Dry Pâté

Type : Undersea mountain
Depth : 40-200m
Species : Tuna, sea bream, marlin
Distance : 15 miles off the coast of Deshaies
Techniques : Trolling, deep jigging

Castle Point

Type : Coastal and shore fishing
Depth : 0-30m
Species : Barracudas, jacks, tarpons
Access : From the shore or by kayak
Best time : Sunrise and sunset

Les Saintes

Type : Archipelago with varied seabeds
Depth : 10-150m
Species : All reef and pelagic species
Special feature : Excellent jigging spots
Access : Boat from Trois-Rivières

Grand Cul-de-Sac Marin

Type : Lagoon and mangroves
Depth : 1-15m
Species : Tarpons, barracudas, jacks
Techniques : Casting and retrieving, sight fishing
Special feature : Ideal spot for beginners

Recommended Fishing Equipment

Fishing Rods

  • Light casting/spinning (7-15 kg) : For coastal casting and retrieving, perfect for barracuda and jacks
  • Jigging rod (100-300g) : For deep vertical fishing
  • Trolling rod (20-50 lbs) : For medium-sized sea bream and tuna
  • Heavy tackle fishing rod (50-130 lbs) : For marlins and large tuna

Reels

  • Spinning 4000-6000 : Versatile for casting and retrieving
  • Jigging reel : High capacity, fast recovery
  • 30-50 drag reel : Robust with good brakes
  • Lever reel : For big game fishing, capacity 400m+ of braided line

Lures and Baits

  • Poppers and stickbaits : 10-20cm for surface fishing
  • Jigs : 80-200g in various colors
  • Trolling lures : Rapala, jig heads
  • Natural bait : Sardines, squid, ballyhoo (orphies)

Leaders and Leaders

  • Braided : PE 2-8 depending on technique (20-80 lbs)
  • Fluorocarbon : 30-150 lbs for leader line
  • Steel wire : Essential for barracudas and wahoo
  • Nylon : 40-100 lbs for longline rigs

Essential Accessories

  • Landing net or boat hook depending on the size of the catch
  • Fish tongs and thread cutter
  • Cooler for storing catches
  • GPS and fish finder to locate spots
  • Life jacket and safety equipment
  • Sun protection: hat, sunscreen, polarized sunglasses

Fishing Regulations in Guadeloupe

Permits and Licenses

No license is required for recreational sea fishing in Guadeloupe. However, you must comply with the quotas and regulations in force..

  • Recreational fishing: Free, no license required
  • Commercial fishing: Professional license required
  • Respect for protected areas and marine reserves

Minimum Catch Sizes

It is imperative to respect minimum sizes in order to preserve stocks :

  • Mahi-mahi: 60 cm
  • Yellowfin tuna : 90 cm
  • Snapper : 30 cm
  • Grouper : 40 cm
  • Marlin: 200 cm (catch and release recommended)
  • Lobsters: 21 cm (from eye to tail)

Recreational Fishing Quotas

  • Maximum 5 kg per fisherman per day for reef fish
  • Maximum 10 kg for pelagic fish
  • No more than 2 fish of the same species
  • Lobsters: 6 per fisherman

Protected Species

These species must never be fished :

  • All sea turtles
  • All marine mammals (dolphins, whales)
  • Manta rays
  • Sharks of certain species
  • Lambi (conch) - moratorium in effect

Protected Areas

  • Cousteau Reserve : No fishing around the Pigeon Islets
  • Grand Cul-de-Sac Marin : Specific regulations, prohibited areas
  • Petite Terre : Nature reserve, fishing strictly prohibited
  • Respect breeding and nursery areas

Best Practices

  • Practice catch and release for large breeding specimens.
  • Use barbless hooks to make it easier to release fish back into the water.
  • Don't waste: only catch what you will consume.
  • Bring back all your trash and fishing line
  • Respect other users of the sea
  • Report any pollution or poaching to the authorities
